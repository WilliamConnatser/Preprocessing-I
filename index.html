About

If you want to get to know me, then I suppose we can start with my name being William, but you can call me any nickname derivative of it you'd like. People that have known me for a long time usually call me Will because that was what I preferred growing up. Nowadays, I don't mind if you call me William, Willy, Bill, or Billy! I am 32 years young from College Station, Texas, and have always been fascinated with technology since I was a pre-teen.

Back in the Windows 95 and DOS days, my mother got our family's first desktop computer. Since the point forward I was always the one the family would come to when they needed help with anything having to do with computers. Ironically, booting into DOS in order to play Dr. Quandry's Island and Word Munchers was not child's play! When I was 14 years old my dad took me into a Half-Price Bookstore and let me pick out any book I wanted. After putting on the best "pleading eye face" that every parent can almost never say no to, my dad reluctantly allowed me to take home "Steal This Computer Book" which is essentially a beginner's guide to becoming a "cypherpunk".

By the time I was 18 years old I had a CompTIA A+ Certification, a year of Java programming, and a year of C++ programming under my belt due to the great programs at A&M Consolidated High School. I was seemingly destined to work with computers, but it turned out that life had a different plan for me. With the help of my dad, I landed a great job that I could not refuse in the oil & gas industry which ultimately steered me away from pursuing a career in technology. After many diverse work experiences in numerous industries, I have ended up gravitating back to my main interests by deciding to pursue a career involving the things I've always bee most passionate about in life- technology and Computer Science.

Work Experience

Several years ago I got interested in getting back into programming, so I spent about a year and a half learning on my own with online tutorials and videos (when I wasn't working at my other full-time job.) Since June of 2018, I have been self-employed and spent all of my time as a software engineer. My primary focus has been on building responsive and reactive full-stack applications. The tools that I am most familiar with include HTML, CSS, Sass, Bootstrap, Javascript, Node, Meteor, MongoDB, Vue, GraphQL.

I have so far served several clients as a freelance web developer by creating responsive and reactive full-stack applications which enable them to make their businesses more efficient. My biggest project to date was helping an oil & gas services company which needed an automated system to organize the workflow and data involving researching the ownership and status of the assets owned by a large ranch in west Texas.

I am also the Managing Director of YOLO International LLC which is an investment company focused on Bitcoin, decentralized technologies, and precious metals. I have served as the lead Software Engineer of multiple full-stack web applications in our portfolio. Our portfolio currently consists of Bitcoin, Cryptocurrencies, Gold, Silver, Domain Names, and Website Assets.
